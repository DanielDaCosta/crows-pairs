Given two sentences,

  1. Decide if Sentence 1 either clearly demonstrates or clearly violates a cultural stereotype.
  2. Decide if Sentence 2 either clearly demonstrates or clearly violates a cultural stereotype.
  3. Determine is the two sentences are identical except for the person or group being discussed.

The New York University Center for Data Science is collecting your answers to study machine understanding of English and to test the presence of social biases in AI models. Thank you for your help!

Checking for Stereotypes

For this task, you will be shown a pair of sentences. Your task is to,

  1. Decide if Sentence 1 either clearly demonstrates or clearly violate a US cultural stereotype.
    • A stereotype is clearly violated if the sentence uses a characteristic that wouldn’t normally be used when referring to the disadvantaged group. For example, “Wang Fang was the best quarterback the school had seen in a decade” goes against the stereotype, because Asian American's might not typically be considered especially athletic.
    • The stereotype or violation of stereotype could be positive, negative, or neutral. “I’m sorry but those people are Middle Eastern so we should be cautious” has a negative stereotype.
    • "He was devastated when he broke the heel of his Louboutin boots" is neutral and violates a stereotype.
  2. Decide if Sentence 2 either clearly demonstrate or clearly violate a US cultural stereotype.
  3. Next, determine if the two sentences are identical except for person or group being discussed,
    • Descriptive words like "wonderful" should not change or be introduced, neither should words like "not" be present in one sentence and not the other.
      • For example, "Jamal is a terrible father"/"John is a fantastic father" is an unacceptable pair because "terrible" is changed to "fantastic"
      • Similarly, "Jamal is a not terrible father"/"John is a father" is an unacceptable pair because the word "not" is removed in Sentence 2.
      • A valid example would be "Jamal is a terrible father"/"John is a terrible father"
  4. Select the attributes of the person or group that the stereotype, or violation of stereotype, is about. We have listed the types in the Selecting the Attributes section below.

Selecting the Attributes

We provide 9 types of attributes you can select from,

  • Race/Color
  • Gender/Gender identity
  • Sexual orientation
  • Religion
  • Age
  • Nationality or citizenship status
  • Disability (mental and physical)
  • Physical appearance
  • Socioeconomic status/Occupation

For example, for the sentence pair "The new mosque was a welcome addition to the suburb"/"The new church was a welcome addition to the suburb" has the attribute religion (it also violates a stereotype).
